# Personal Site Strategy

## Target problem

People who encounter Adolfo online need to understand who he is, what he has done, and where to find him within a few seconds. The hard part is making that feel current and credible without turning the site into a dense portfolio or conventional resume page.

## Our approach

The site should lead with distinct visual taste while keeping the experience minimal, one-page, and scannable in a single scroll. It will prioritize identity, credibility, and contact paths over deep case studies, long writing, or a conventional multi-page resume.

## Who it's for

**Primary:** Startup community contacts - They're hiring the site to quickly understand who Adolfo is, what kind of work he has done, and where to continue the conversation after meeting or hearing about him.

## Key metrics

- **Site health** - The page loads cleanly with no broken visual assets, broken links, or obvious rendering issues.
- **Company credibility** - Company logos and experience references render correctly and support the credibility of the resume section.
- **Protected content** - Any authenticated or private section remains properly gated.
- **Taste signal** - The page feels clean, polished, and intentionally minimal on desktop and mobile.

## Tracks

### Visual direction and interaction polish

Define and apply the minimal aesthetic, layout rhythm, typography, and interaction details that make the site feel current and intentional.

_Why it serves the approach:_ Distinct visual taste is the main signal, so the site has to feel polished before visitors read every detail.

### Content and resume structure

Shape the one-page resume and identity content so the key information is visible in a single scroll.

_Why it serves the approach:_ The site needs to answer "who is Adolfo?" quickly without becoming a dense CV or portfolio archive.

### Asset and link reliability

Audit and fix company logos, external links, social links, and any brittle assets in the current site.

_Why it serves the approach:_ Broken logos or links undermine credibility faster than missing content does.

### Protected/private section behavior

Verify that any authenticated or private section is properly protected and does not leak into the public experience.

_Why it serves the approach:_ The public site should stay clean and minimal while preserving boundaries around anything private.
